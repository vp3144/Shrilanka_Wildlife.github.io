Mammals of Sri Lanka

Asian elephant, Dugong, Gray slender loris, Purple-faced langur, Grizzled giant squirrel, Indian hare

There are 125 mammal species in Sri Lanka, of which one is critically endangered, ten are endangered, ten are vulnerable, and three are near threatened.

Reptiles of Sri Lanka

turtles, crocodilians, snakes, amphisbaenians, lizards, tuatara

The reptilian diversity in Sri Lanka is somewhat higher than other vertebrates such as mammals and fish currently with 181 reptile species.

Amphibians of Sri Lanka

Kandyan Dwarf Toad, Indian Skipper frog, Sri Lankan Painted Frog, Sri Lankan Golden-backed frog, Montane Hour-glass Tree frog

Sri Lanka is host to over 120+ species of amphibians, of which over 90 species is being endemic to the country.The 85% of endemicity ratio makes Sri Lanka the country which has the highest amphibian endemism in Asia. During the past decade the finding of new amphibians in Sri Lanka has increased.

Birds of Sri Lanka

Fulvous whistling-duck, Sri Lanka spurfowl, Greater flamingo, Little grebe, Asian emerald dove, Green-billed coucal, Sri Lanka frogmouth

The bird life of Sri Lanka is very rich for its size and 505 species have been recorded. 34 species are endemic. The other resident species are also found in the nearby Indian mainland, but over 80 have developed distinct Sri Lankan races.

Freshwater Fish of Sri Lanka

Asian elephant, Common spiny loach, Banded Mountain Loach, Barred danio, Black ruby barb, Sinhala barb

There are 95 species of freshwater fish occur in the country, where 53 of those are endemic. 41% of all known species of fish of Sri Lanka are found in freshwater. There are about 70% of endemism of those fish. Most of them are listed into IUCN categories.

Butterflies of Sri Lanka

Ceylon Tree-Nymph, Ceylon Tiger, Ceylon Palmfly, Ceylon Treebrown, Pale Ceylon Six Lineblue, Black Flat

The majority of species are found in the foothills (up to 3,000 feet (910 m) elevation). A much smaller number of species are found above 4,000 feet (1,200 m), while 20 species of butterfly are restricted to the low lying dry zone (below 500 feet (150 m) elevation).
